## Fit-to-build read (the team's lens)

The build side is trivially in the wheelhouse — a Next.js/Supabase/OpenAI app the team could write from scratch in weeks, with an obvious engineering roadmap (Store Connect upload, localization, Play Store). That is precisely the problem: the code is worth little, the category is crowded with better-funded and free alternatives, and 100% of the value creation left is audience-building and content marketing for indie developers — the founder's X-and-listicles game, not this team's.

## Does the value transfer? (key-person check)

TrustMRR lists the founder's X follower count as a headline stat — the audience *is* the asset, and it doesn't convey. What transfers: code, domain (DR 2), 30 subscriptions of unknown remaining lifetime, and a few listicle backlinks. The honest transfer question is retention: how much of the $405 MRR survives 90 days under an owner who isn't posting about it?

## What we know vs. what we're assuming

- **Verified (buyer-reconciled):** the price cut ($9,000 on Jul 14 shortlist → $5,000 by Jul 18); the ARPU/tier mismatch ($13.50 vs $19 minimum); the competitive saturation; MRR-vs-revenue growth divergence.
- **Assumed / marketplace-attested:** all dollar figures (Stripe badge); 85% margin; 30 subs; product quality (site blocked inspection).
- **Unknown (and how it swings):** cohort retention (the deal-decider — good retention ≈ +8-10 merit pts, still PASS-capped by channel); channel mix (could flip growth_lever_fits, unlikely at DR 2); cofounder sign-off; the 3 competing offers; price flexibility to ≤$3,750.
