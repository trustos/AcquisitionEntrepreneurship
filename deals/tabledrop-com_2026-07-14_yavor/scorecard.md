# Acquisition Scorecard — TableDrop.com

**Verdict:** PASS (provisional) — 44.2/100 (completeness 49%)  ·  analyst: Yavor

Right shape (a real UK web+mobile hospitality SaaS you could operate) but the growth lever is B2B restaurant/bar sales — your weakness — in a brutally saturated market owned by POS incumbents (Toast/Square/SpotOn) and specialists (Sunday, me&u); it's order-management-only (no payments), shows no disclosed traction, and is just over budget. growth_lever_fits = false → PASS.

## Scores
- operability_upside: 3/5 (med) Real web + native mobile SaaS on your stack — you can run/maintain it. But it's a mature commodity: little product you could add would move the needle; the bottleneck is sales, not features.
## Scores
- pmf_retention: 2/5 (low) No customers/revenue disclosed; 'domain + software for $4k' phrasing implies ~zero traction. Restaurant tools also churn hard. Unverified — key unknown.
## Scores
- unit_economics: 2/5 (low) Model is fine ($49.99/mo or $399.99/yr per venue, recurring) — but current revenue is unknown and almost certainly negligible.
## Scores
- moat_defensibility: 1/5 (med) Near-zero moat. Commodity QR ordering; NO payments (it rides your existing till), so it loses to POS-bundled QR from Toast/Square/SpotOn and to Sunday/me&u. 'A lot of QR menus are just a PDF + sticker.'
## Scores
- growth_market: 2/5 (med) Digital ordering is growing, but this segment is saturated and owned by POS incumbents; a standalone no-payments tool is on the wrong side of that.
## Scores
- tech_ip: 3/5 (med) Real transferable asset (web + iOS + Android apps + backend + domain, UK Ltd) — more than a content site. But native apps mean ongoing maintenance + App Store/Play account transfer to confirm.
## Scores
- transfer_key_person: 3/5 (low) Assets (code/domain/apps) are impersonal and transfer — but with ~no customer base there's little growth engine to inherit either way.
## Scores
- deal_terms: 2/5 (med) $4,000 for a no-revenue commodity codebase a team like yours could rebuild — poor value — and it's over the $3k budget (1.3x).
## Scores
- seller_adverse_selection: 2/5 (med) 'Domain + software for sale', no revenue mentioned = built-but-couldn't-sell in a hard market. Not fraud; just likely no traction.

## Key unknowns
- Any paying venues / MRR at all? (couldn't read the Reddit body — my fetch tool is blocked from reddit.com). If there's real, growing MRR with a repeatable channel it changes the picture; the phrasing suggests not.
- Why selling, and what's included beyond domain + code (the iOS/Android app-store listings + accounts? the RoomDrop sibling? the TableDrop LTD entity?).
- Is there ANY non-sales acquisition channel (a POS partnership, a directory, inbound) — or is it 100% cold B2B hospitality sales?
- Code/IP ownership + clean transfer of the App Store / Google Play developer accounts.
- Ongoing costs (hosting, app-store fees, any third-party APIs).

## Seller questions
1. How many paying venues are live today, MRR, and churn — screen-shared from Stripe/billing? (PMF + revenue; the decisive unknown)
2. Why are you selling, and how did you acquire the venues you have (cold sales, a POS partner, inbound)? (channel — does any non-sales lever exist?)
3. Exactly what's included: domain, full codebase, the iOS + Android apps AND their developer accounts, the RoomDrop sibling, the TableDrop LTD company? (IP/transfer)
4. Is 100% of the code and design owned by you (no license-limited templates), and will the app-store accounts transfer? (IP)
5. Monthly running costs and any third-party dependencies? (unit economics)
6. Any existing POS/payment integrations or partnerships that would help distribution? (moat/channel)
