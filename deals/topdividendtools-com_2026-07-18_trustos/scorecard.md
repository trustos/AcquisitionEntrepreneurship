## Fit-to-build read (the team's lens)

There is nothing to build. The 'product' is a filterable table; the business is publishing cadence, audience growth on X, and selling banner spots to fintech marketers. Every hour an owner puts in is a content-and-sales hour. This is the single worst fit-to-build in the entire deal corpus — worse than the SaaS PASSes, because those at least contained software.

## Does the value transfer? (key-person check)

The demand engine is a named X persona ('Top Dividend ETFs') whose email domain even hosts the site's business contact. If the account conveys, the buyer inherits a content treadmill they can't feed; if it doesn't, they buy a DR-22 domain and a table of links. Either branch fails the team.

## What we know vs. what we're assuming

- **Verified (buyer-reconciled):** the 24x gap between badge multiple and recurring multiple; the one-week $4,164 revenue burst; negative 30d growth; the monetization model (affiliate + sponsorships + paid submissions); the X-brand linkage; business-type classification (content/media).
- **Assumed / marketplace-attested:** all dollar figures (Stripe badge); 14 subs; ~1,000 users; DR 22.
- **Unknown (and how it swings):** revenue composition by product (Stripe export — expected ~97% one-time); X account inclusion; traffic sources; sponsor renewals (the only possible upside surprise); what the 14 subs are. None of it flips the classification gate.
