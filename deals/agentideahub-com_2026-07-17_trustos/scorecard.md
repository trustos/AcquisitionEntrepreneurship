# Acquisition Scorecard — AgentIdeaHub.com

**Verdict:** PASS (provisional) — 28.8/100 (completeness 80%)  ·  analyst: trustos

First in-budget deal ($2k vs $3k) but a verified spike-and-flip: a 3-week-old domain sold as a 14-month business (whois 2026-06-27 vs claimed May-2025 founding), the $1,422 'spike month' is ~29 x $49 lifetime deals with $0 in the last 7 days, the '$78 MRR / 19 subs' cannot come from the site's own no-subscription pricing, the live site is fronted by a DIFFERENT person (@iamfra5er) than the seller (@mattmerrick16), and the only acquisition channel is the seller's personal X audience - which does not convey.

## Scores
- operability_upside: 3/5 (med) Next.js 15/Supabase/Stripe one-pager - trivially operable; but it's a content template, not a product: upside requires audience-building, not engineering.
## Scores
- pmf_retention: 1/5 (high) No recurring product exists: site sells a $49 one-time lifetime deal ('No subscriptions'); the spike month (~29 LTD sales) came from an audience blast, $0 in last 7d; the '19 subs/$78 MRR' cannot come from this pricing page (provenance unknown, MRR -45%/30d).
## Scores
- unit_economics: 1/5 (med) LTD-only economics: revenue already collected by seller, buyer inherits lifetime-access + future-update obligations to ~29-50 customers with $0 attached; 95% margin is meaningless with no recurring engine.
## Scores
- moat_defensibility: 1/5 (high) 3-week-old template site, DR 0, 1 page indexed, gated unverifiable (plausibly AI-generated) content; free substitutes everywhere (GitHub lists, IdeaBrowser at scale, ChatGPT).
## Scores
- growth_market: 2/5 (med) AI-agent interest is a genuine tailwind, but this slice (paid idea lists) is brand/audience-attached; without a distribution engine there is no market access.
## Scores
- tech_ip: 3/5 (low) Modern minimal stack transfers easily; content provenance unverified; the live site's operator-of-record ambiguity (@iamfra5er) clouds what IP is actually conveyed.
## Scores
- transfer_key_person: 1/5 (high) The only channel is the seller's (or Fraser's) personal X audience; zero SEO, no email capture, no organic loop - the growth engine walks away at closing. Site literally tells customers to contact a different person than the seller.
## Scores
- deal_terms: 1/5 (high) $2,000 = ~2.1x a declining true ARR ($936) or ~1.4x a one-time spike month; category comps for sub-$100-MRR directories clear at $50-500; the 0.1x headline is spike-annualization. Plus inherited LTD obligations.
## Scores
- seller_adverse_selection: 0/5 (high) Verified worst-case: serial flipper (runs under2k.net, a flip marketplace; 10 near-$0 projects; 2 simultaneous listings) flipping days after an LTD spike, 'need the funds fast', fake scarcity countdown, domain 3 weeks old sold as 14 months, seller is not the site's public operator.

## Key unknowns
- Who actually owns/operates the site today - the footer says @iamfra5er (Fraser), the seller is @mattmerrick16, and whois updated the day after listing; if already sold/encumbered the listing is stale (deal-breaker).
- Provenance of the '$78 MRR / 19 subscriptions' - the site sells no subscription; if from another product on the same Stripe account, the listed metrics misattribute revenue (confirms 'contradicted').
- What the claimed May-2025 founding refers to, given the domain was registered 2026-06-27 - is any of the $2,431 all-time revenue attributable to THIS asset?
- Content provenance: are the '257 curated ideas / 25+ guides' original work or AI-generated filler (affects the only real asset being sold).
- Whether the ~29 LTD buyers' lifetime obligations (access + 'all future updates') transfer contractually to the buyer.
