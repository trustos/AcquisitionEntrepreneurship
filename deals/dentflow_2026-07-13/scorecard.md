# Acquisition Scorecard — DentFlow

**Verdict:** PASS — 43.6/100 (completeness 66%)

The first verified-revenue, trivially-priced ($169) target and the closest to interesting — but as a solo asset-buy it's a near-zero-traction UK dental-finance niche where the value levers are dental domain expertise and B2B distribution you lack. It becomes a cheap Investigate-worthy punt ONLY if the dentist-founder commits to partnering.

## Scores
- build_scale_fit: 2/5 (med) The app itself is trivial for the team to own/run. But the value levers are UK dental-domain knowledge (CPD rules, tax, associate pay) + niche B2B distribution into dentistry — both team gaps. The bottleneck isn't code.
- pmf_retention: 1/5 (high) Stripe-verified but tiny: $30 MRR, 2 subs after 6 months. 'Positive feedback, no real marketing.' PMF essentially unproven.
- unit_economics: 2/5 (med) Good MODEL (B2B recurring, GBP14/mo, professionals who pay for tax/time savings) but negligible current revenue; LTV/CoCA unproven.
- moat_channel: 2/5 (med) DR 0, LinkedIn only, no moat yet. A defined vertical niche is a targeting plus; potential moat (dental-community trust + workflow depth) is exactly what the team can't build without the founder.
- growth_market: 2/5 (med) Real but small single-country vertical (UK associate dentists). Expansion (other countries/professions) each needs fresh domain work.
- tech_ip: 3/5 (med) Simple web app + Stripe, UK GDPR compliant, transferable. Holding dentists' financial data carries a compliance/security burden.
- deal_quality: 4/5 (med) $169 is essentially free and low-risk on money; founder offers onboarding/support + 'consider partnership' (favorable terms). Only ding: cheap because there's ~nothing there yet; 8 competing offers.
- seller_risk: 3/5 (med) Transparent, identifiable (a real UK dentist), Stripe-verified, honest about tiny traction. Low deception risk; mild adverse selection (nice idea that didn't take off).

## Key unknowns
- Will the dentist-founder actually partner (domain + dental-community distribution) — the single factor that flips competence from fail to viable. Without him you're software devs guessing at UK dental finance with no channel.
- Real usage beyond 2 paid: free/active users, trial->paid, and what the 'positive feedback' concretely asked for.
- Is there any repeatable channel into UK associate dentists (BDA, CPD providers, dental forums), or was it all the founder's personal network?
- How much UK-specific domain logic is baked in (CPD hours, HMRC/tax, associate pay), and the compliance load of holding dentists' financial data.
- Full IP/data ownership and transferability; stack/hosting.

## Seller questions
1. Would you stay on as a domain + distribution PARTNER (rev-share/equity) — we're strong engineers but not dentists or UK dental marketers? On what terms? (decisive: competence hinges on this)
2. Beyond the 2 paying subs: how many free/active users, what's trial->paid, and what specifically did the 'positive feedback' ask you to build? (PMF)
3. How did the first users find DentFlow, and what channels realistically reach UK associate dentists (associations, CPD providers, dental forums/FB)? (channel)
4. What UK-specific logic is built in (CPD rules, tax/HMRC, associate pay structures), and what compliance obligations come with holding dentists' financial data (GDPR/data processing)? (domain + tech)
5. Is 100% of code, domain, and user data owned by you and transferable? Any contractors? What's the stack and hosting? (IP/transfer)
6. Why sell at $169 after 6 months, and what are the 8 offers/other interested buyers? (adverse selection + competition)
