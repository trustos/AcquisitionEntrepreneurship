## Fit-to-build read (the team's lens)

Half of this deal fits beautifully: the stack is the team's exact stack, and the roadmap (more integrations, more countries' e-invoicing formats, better bank matching) is pure engineering. The other half doesn't: customers arrive via content marketing, SEO, and the founder's personal indie-hacker audience — none of which transfer or fit the team — and the Italy-specific compliance features need Italian domain knowledge and support. Buying it means inheriting a content-marketing job plus a compliance beat, with the engineering already mostly done.

## Does the value transfer? (key-person check)

This is the crux. The founder runs a four-product studio, his own products are the showcase customers, and his audience is the distribution. What verifiably transfers: code, domain (DR 9), Stripe billing, and whichever subscribers are arm's-length — which is exactly the unverified part. An MRR-retention earnout would price this risk correctly if the deal were pursued.

## What we know vs. what we're assuming

- **Verified (buyer-reconciled):** product is live and substantial (Gmail/Outlook/IMAP extraction, bank matching, QBO/Xero integrations, 4 languages); founder identity and studio; 3 of 5 showcase logos are the founder's own products; figures internally consistent; competitive field crowded and commoditizing.
- **Assumed / marketplace-attested:** all dollar figures (Stripe-attested badge); 85% margin; +53%/30d growth; 'blog/content/SEO' as the channel.
- **Unknown (and how it swings):** subscriber identities (the deal-decider); Google OAuth restricted-scope verification transfer; SDI intermediary contract; churn to date; price flexibility. Best realistic case (all subs arm's-length, OAuth transfers, price ≤$3,750) ≈ MARGINAL~55, provisional; worst case confirms PASS.
