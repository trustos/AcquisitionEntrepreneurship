# Acquisition Scorecard — Synta

**Verdict:** MARGINAL — 56.0/100 (completeness 76%)

A GENUINE business (verified ~$12k MRR, 260 paying subs, ~$113k trailing-year, YC-backed) in your exact domain — but declining ~20%+ from its March peak with an eroding moat (n8n's own AI + free open-source n8n MCP servers), and asking $100,000 (~33x your budget). The right shape, wrong price and wrong trajectory: a turnaround you can't currently afford.

## Scores
- build_scale_fit: 4/5 (med) Your exact wheelhouse (AI/MCP/n8n on Next/TS/Anthropic/GCP). You could operate and improve it; the turnaround lever (reverse churn, differentiate, extend beyond n8n) leans on product skill.
- pmf_retention: 3/5 (high) REAL PMF: 260 paying subs, ~$12k MRR (verified). But declining — MRR growth -14% (30d), revenue down from a $16.9k March peak to ~$13k. Proven, but eroding.
- unit_economics: 3/5 (high) $12k MRR, 92% gross margin, $34/$67 plans, ~$47 ARPU. Healthy recurring model; watch Anthropic/GCP cost pass-through and the decline.
- moat_channel: 2/5 (med) Thin, eroding moat: n8n ships its own AI and free open-source n8n MCP servers exist. YC brand + 16k users + content/SEO (DR 13) give some edge, but the decline shows it's being competed away.
- growth_market: 2/5 (high) Hot market (AI workflow automation) but this product is on the wrong side of it — declining ~20% from peak as the platform it depends on commoditizes the function.
- tech_ip: 3/5 (med) Modern wheelhouse stack, YC-grade eng likely, knowledge-base asset, transferable. Verify IP + user-list transfer.
- deal_quality: 2/5 (med) $100,000 at 0.7x trailing revenue is a fair-to-cheap SaaS multiple in the abstract — but it's ~33x the team's budget (not actionable without capital), the multiple flatters a declining asset (forward multiple is worse), and 21 offers = competition.
- seller_risk: 2/5 (med) Selling as revenue declines ~20% off peak. Reason cited: other opportunities + personal (family bereavement) — sympathetic and plausible, low deception (verified revenue), but the timing is classic adverse selection.

## Key unknowns
- Cause and trajectory of the decline: is churn accelerating, and what's driving it (n8n native AI? a competitor? a pricing change)? Decisive for a turnaround thesis.
- Can the team even fund $100k, or negotiate seller financing / an earnout tied to retention? Without capital this is moot regardless of fit.
- Defensibility vs n8n's own AI + free open-source n8n MCP servers — is there a durable reason to keep paying?
- Real churn + net revenue retention on the 260 paying subs, and cohort trend.
- What transfers: 100% code + the 800+ node knowledge base + the 16k user/email list + domain; API-cost pass-through and true margin at scale.

## Seller questions
1. Revenue peaked ~$16.9k in March and is down to ~$13k — what's driving the decline, and is churn accelerating? (the turnaround crux)
2. Are you open to seller financing or an earnout tied to retained revenue, given the recent trend? (deal structure — the only way $100k works for us)
3. How do you defend against n8n's own AI features and free open-source n8n MCP servers? (moat)
4. Paying-subscriber monthly churn and net revenue retention, with cohort trend? (retention)
5. What exactly transfers — 100% code, the 800+ node knowledge base, the 16k user/email list, domain — and any license/API-cost pass-through? (IP/transfer)
6. Per-active-user Anthropic + GCP cost; is 92% margin durable at scale? (unit economics)
7. What are the '2 potential B2B customers ready to onboard' worth, and why sell now beyond the personal reasons? (upside + adverse selection)
