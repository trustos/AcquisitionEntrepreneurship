## The honest headline first

I found plenty of real listings — but here's the pattern you need to see: **almost every sub-$3k software product, even the ones with real revenue, is for sale for the same reason — it works but "doesn't get traffic."** That's not a coincidence; it's the definition of the price band. The founders who could market them didn't have to sell at $2k. So the recurring growth lever on these is *marketing / ASO / SEO / outreach* — your stated weakness. The evaluator will keep flagging that, correctly.

So the game at this budget isn't "find a gem that runs itself." It's picking the ones where **either (a) the revenue is genuinely verified so you're only buying a distribution problem, not a fiction, or (b) some distribution already exists (organic traffic, an app-store ranking, a community) that you can grow with product work rather than cold marketing.**
