I can't log into these, so I can't verify live contents. These are the ones repeatedly cited as active for buying/selling small software. **Names/URLs drift — search rather than trust a link.**

### Facebook groups
Honest caveat: FB buy/sell-*website* groups skew heavily to **content/affiliate sites** (your weakness); the SaaS-labelled groups are more *community* than marketplace, but deals do get posted. Search these names on Facebook:
- **Niche Website Flippers** — the largest, but content-site heavy.
- **SaaS Growth Hacks** (~31k), **SaaS Revolutionaries** (~7k), **SaaS Lifetime Deals** (~12k), **Scaling SaaS Founders**, **B2B Entrepreneurs** (~17k) — community-first; watch for "for sale" posts.
- Generic buy/sell: search **"Buy Sell Websites"**, **"Website Flipping"**, **"Flippa Buyers and Sellers"**, **"Micro Acquisitions"**.

## How to use this (given your constraints)
1. **Live where the numbers are real:** check **TrustMRR** first every time — verified revenue removes the biggest risk at this price band.
2. **Automate inflow:** subscribe to the TrustMRR Telegram bot + Microns/Acquire newsletters so you're not manually trawling.
3. **Skim, don't dwell, in FB/Reddit:** they're noisy and content-heavy; use them to catch the occasional real micro-SaaS, not as your main feed.
4. **Run anything promising through `acquisition-evaluator`** before you get attached — especially the free-listing finds, which self-select for the "works but needs marketing" trap.
