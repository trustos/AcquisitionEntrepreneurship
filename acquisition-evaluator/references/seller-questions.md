# Seller due-diligence question bank

Draw from this bank in Phase 6. Don't dump the whole list — select and tailor the questions that resolve *this* target's specific unknowns and red flags, phrase them for the actual product, and order them deal-breakers first. Tag each with the rubric dimension it resolves and a one-line "why we're asking" so the user understands what each answer buys.

Ask the seller only what the seller uniquely knows or can evidence. Things the user or public research can answer shouldn't be here.

## Revenue & financials (verify the money)

- Can you share the last 24 months of revenue by month, exported from Stripe/Paddle/the payment processor (not a spreadsheet you typed)? *— Verifies revenue at the source; the top defense against inflated numbers.*
- Can we reconcile that against bank statements for the same period? *— Confirms the money actually landed.*
- What's the split of recurring vs one-time revenue, and monthly vs annual plans? *— Recurring is worth more; annual prepayments flatter cash.*
- What are the true monthly costs (hosting, APIs, tools, contractors, support) with invoices? *— Cost base is the easiest place to massage SDE/profit.*
- Are there any refunds, chargebacks, discounts, or one-off deals inflating the trailing numbers? *— Strips out non-repeatable revenue.*

## Retention & customers (do they stay?)

- What's monthly and annual churn, and can you show a cohort retention chart? *— Retention is the whole game for subscriptions.*
- Net revenue retention over the last year? *— Above 100% means the base grows itself.*
- Customer concentration: what % of revenue is the top 1, 5, and 10 customers? *— One big customer leaving can sink a small business.*
- Any customers on non-standard contracts, or with change-of-control clauses that trigger on sale? *— They can walk the moment you buy.*

## Acquisition channel & marketing (can WE keep the customers coming?)

- Where do new customers actually come from — organic, word of mouth, SEO, paid ads, integrations, a directory/marketplace? Rough % by channel. *— A single marketing channel the team can't run is a fragility gate for us.*
- If SEO: read-access to Google Search Console, and have you ever had a manual penalty or a core-update traffic drop? *— SEO-dependent businesses are one algorithm change from trouble.*
- If paid: current CAC, blended ROAS, and what happens to signups when you pause spend? *— Tests whether growth survives without a marketer.*
- What's currently driving growth (or decline), and what has stopped working? *— Surfaces a decline the seller can already feel.*

## Product, tech & IP (can we own and modernize it?)

- Is 100% of the code and all assets owned by the selling entity (no contractor-owned IP, no un-relicensable dependencies)? *— IP/transfer hard gate.*
- Architecture overview, repo access under NDA, test coverage, and any known critical tech debt or security issues? *— Scopes modernization; guards against a forced rewrite.*
- Any hard dependence on a single third-party API/model/vendor, and what's the exposure if its price or terms change? *— Platform/API risk (see Apollo, Amazon Associates).*
- What's the real ongoing maintenance and on-call load? *— "Buying a job" check.*

## Operations & transfer (what breaks on handover?)

- What does running this actually take weekly — support tickets, manual tasks, content updates — and who does them today? *— Reveals hidden ops load and key-person risk.*
- Can all critical accounts transfer: domain/registrar, hosting, repos, analytics (GA4/Search Console), email/ESP, app-store & developer accounts, ad and affiliate accounts, trademarks? *— Transferability check.*
- Payment processor: since Stripe/PayPal usually can't transfer, what's the plan for billing migration and re-subscribing customers? *— Plan the revenue-continuity gap.*
- Will you provide a transition period and documented SOPs? *— De-risks handover; MIT data shows acquired-team attrition is high.*

## Deal & the real reason for sale

- Why are you selling, and why now? *— The single most important question; assume you know less than the seller.*
- Are you open to seller financing, an earnout tied to customer retention, or a holdback in escrow? *— Willingness shifts risk back to the informed party; refusal is itself a signal.*
- Are there other bidders / what's your timeline? *— Reads urgency and competition.*
- Any pending legal, tax, IP, privacy (GDPR/CCPA), or platform-policy issues, past or present? *— Uncovers liabilities you'd inherit.*
- What would you do to grow this if you were staying? *— Free strategic insight and a tell about untapped upside or hidden ceilings.*

## Tailoring notes

- For **content/affiliate/e-commerce** targets, lead hard with the channel and concentration questions — that's where these businesses (and this team's ability to run them) live or die.
- For **mobile apps**, add: App Store/Play ranks and review trend, % revenue via in-app purchase (and the 15–30% platform cut), and ASO dependence.
- For **AI-wrapper products**, probe the moat beyond the underlying model and the margin exposure if model prices change.

## Transfer / key-person — does the value survive your exit?  *(v2)*

- What actually drives new customers today, and how much of it is *you* (your audience, relationships, reputation) versus the product/brand/SEO? *— resolves: transfer/key-person; the #2 question after "can we run it" — personal distribution may not convey.*
- Would you stay on for a transition, or as an advisor/partner, and on what terms? *— the fix for founder-dependent value.*
- Which audiences and integrations transfer in writing (domain, analytics, ad/affiliate, app-store, social handles, email list, partner relationships)? *— nail down what conveys.*

## Deal structure & fit  *(v2)*

- Asking price and its basis; are you open to seller financing, a retention-linked earnout, or a holdback? *— structure bridges price and shifts risk; refusal is itself a signal.*
- Why priced at this multiple, and why sell now? *— interrogate a cheap multiple; it usually hides decline.*
